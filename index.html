Financial Modeling

We use the **Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)** to assess the expected return on our investments.

$$ E(R_i) = R_f + \beta_i (E(R_m) - R_f) $$

Where:

  • $E(R_i)$ is the expected return on the asset.
  • $R_f$ is the risk-free rate of return.
  • $\beta_i$ (beta) is the volatility of the asset relative to the market.
  • $E(R_m)$ is the expected return of the market.
This equation helps us determine if a new investment offers a suitable return for the level of risk involved. It's a fundamental part of our portfolio management strategy.
